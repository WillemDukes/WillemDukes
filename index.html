Environmental Education & Trail Signage System

Eagle Scout Service Project | Fish River Nature Preserve

As a junior in high school, I partnered with the South Alabama Land Trust (SALT) to design and implement a signage system for the Fish River Nature Preserve in Fairhope, AL. The goal of the project was to improve visitor understanding of local ecosystems while providing clear navigation throughout the trail.

I developed two primary types of signage: 5 ecosystem information boards describing the different ecological zones within the preserve, and 15 plant identification signs highlighting native and invasive species.

At the beginning of the project, I conducted on-site trail walkthroughs to collect GIS data. I used this data to create a detailed digital trail map using Google My Maps, complete with waypoints corresponding to each sign location. This map supported both installation planning and future trail management.

I coordinated directly with Pulse Design to finalize sign layouts and materials, prioritizing durability and readability in an outdoor setting. Once fabrication was complete, I recruited and led volunteer teams to install the signage along the 1.6-mile trail, managing logistics, safety, and workflow throughout the installation process.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Project planning & execution across multiple phases and stakeholders
  • Leadership & volunteer coordination in a real-world outdoor environment
  • Stakeholder communication with land trust staff and design professionals
  • GIS data collection & spatial mapping for trail planning and signage placement
  • Public-facing technical communication for environmental education

Outcome: Successfully delivered a fully mapped 1.6-mile trail system featuring over 20 professionally designed signs. The project provided visitor navigation, increased public awareness of local ecology and invasive species, and provided SALT with durable, long-term educational infrastructure.
