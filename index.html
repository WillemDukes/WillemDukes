Environmental Education & Trail Signage System

Context: Fish River Nature Preserve, managed by the South Alabama Land Trust, lacked cohesive educational signage to guide visitors and educate them about native and invasive species.

Problem: Visitors had no clear trail map or information about ecological zones and species, limiting educational value and awareness.

Constraints: Budget limitations, need for durable materials, coordination with SALT and sign manufacturers, volunteer availability.

Solution: Designed and installed a series of signs, including a trailhead map, ecosystem markers and plant identification posts. Managed volunteers to construct and install signage while coordinating with SALT's design and GIS teams.

Skills Demonstrated:

  • Project planning & leadership
  • Stakeholder communication
  • Environmental systems analysis
  • Public‑facing technical writing
  • Volunteer coordination

Outcome: Created a mapped 1.6‑mile nature trail with over 15 plant identification signs and educational boards. Improved public knowledge of local ecology and invasive species.

System Dynamics Model Project

Approach: Built a stock‑and‑flow model using Stella Architect, defined system boundaries and incorporated stocks, flows and feedback loops. Generated behaviour‑over‑time graphs to identify key leverage points.
