Environmental Education & Trail Signage System

Eagle Scout Service Project | Fish River Nature Preserve

As a junior in high school, I partnered with the South Alabama Land Trust (SALT) to design and implement a permanent educational signage system for the Fish River Nature Preserve in Fairhope, Alabama. The goal of the project was to improve visitor understanding of local ecosystems while providing clear navigation throughout the trail.

I conducted on-site trail walkthroughs to collect GIS data, which I used to create a detailed digital trail map in Google My Maps. The map included waypoints corresponding to each sign location and supported both installation planning and future trail management.

I developed two primary types of signage: five ecosystem information boards describing distinct ecological zones, and fifteen plant identification signs highlighting native and invasive species throughout the preserve.

I coordinated directly with Pulse Design to finalize sign layouts and materials, prioritizing durability and readability in an outdoor environment. After fabrication, I recruited and led volunteer teams to install the signage along the 1.6-mile trail, managing logistics, safety, and workflow throughout the process.
